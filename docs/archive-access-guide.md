# Ohlone Language Archives & Audio Sources

A comprehensive guide to every known repository of Ohlone language materials, how to access them, and how audio recordings map to the written orthography.

## 1. Audio Recordings — Master Catalog

### 1.1 Harrington Wax Cylinder Recordings (1920s–1930s)

The single most important audio archive for Ohlone languages.

**Recorder:** John Peabody Harrington, Bureau of American Ethnology, Smithsonian Institution

**Speakers recorded:**
| Speaker | Language | Dates | Location | Notes |
|---------|----------|-------|----------|-------|
| María de los Angeles Colás ("Angela") | Chochenyo | Aug 1921 | Pleasanton, CA | Principal Chochenyo informant |
| José Guzmán | Chochenyo | 1920s | Niles, CA | Last fluent speaker (d. 1934). Wax cylinders of speech & songs |
| Ascención Solórsano de Cervantes | Mutsun | Jan 1922, 1929–1930 | Gilroy, CA | Last fluent speaker (d. 1930). "Astonishing" knowledge per Harrington |
| Isabel Meadows | Rumsen | 1932–1936 | Various; Washington D.C. | Last 5 years of her life spent being recorded. Born in Carmel Valley |
| Manuel Panocha | Rumsen | 1930s | CA | Oral history recorded in Spanish |
| Henry Cervantes | Mutsun/Rumsen | 1932 | Berkeley to San Juan Capistrano | Placename trip recordings |
| Marta Herrera | Costanoan | 1932 | Same trip | |

**Format:** Wax cylinders, later aluminum discs. Many have been digitized.

**How to access:**
- **Online (digitized microfilm):** Smithsonian National Anthropological Archives
  - Part 1: https://sova.si.edu — Search "Harrington Costanoan"
  - Part 2: Referenced as NAA.1976-95, Subseries 2.13
  - Part 3: Reels 36-80
- **Email request:** naa@si.edu — Contact the National Anthropological Archives
- **In person:** National Museum of Natural History, Smithsonian Institution, 10th St. & Constitution Ave. NW, Washington, D.C. 20560
- **Note:** Audio wax cylinder recordings and photographs are NOT available online. Only the microfilmed field notes (textual) are digitized. You must contact NAA directly for audio access.

## 2. Written Archives — Master Catalog

### 2.1 Smithsonian National Anthropological Archives

**Collection:** John Peabody Harrington papers: Costanoan
**Extent:** Microfilm reels 1-80+ (three parts)
**Content:**
- Linguistic field notes (Chochenyo, Mutsun, Rumsen)
- Vocabulary organized into rudimentary dictionaries
- Rehearings of historical vocabularies by Arroyo, Merriam, Henshaw, Kroeber, etc.
- Ethnographic notes, placenames, tribal names
- Sketch maps, photographs (not digitized)
- Botanical samples (not digitized)
- Diary of placename trips

**Finding Aids:**
- Part 1: https://sova.si.edu/record/NAA.1976-95 (search for subseries 2.13)
- Smithsonian Learning Lab: https://learninglab.si.edu/resources/view/216584

**How to access:**
1. **Remote:** Email naa@si.edu requesting access to Harrington Costanoan papers
2. **SOVA search:** https://sova.si.edu — search "Harrington Costanoan"
3. **In person:** National Museum of Natural History, Washington D.C.
4. **Microfilm purchase:** Ask NAA about obtaining microfilm copies
5. **Digitized text:** Some microfilm reels have been digitized. Check SOVA for availability.

## 3. Sound-to-Writing Mapping

### 3.1 How Harrington Wrote What He Heard

Harrington used his own phonetic notation system, which differs from both modern IPA and the community orthographies used today. Understanding his notation is critical for interpreting the archival recordings.

**Harrington's notation → Modern Mutsun orthography:**

| Harrington | Modern Mutsun | IPA | Sound |
|-----------|---------------|-----|-------|
| t. | T | /ʈ/ | Retroflex stop |
| s. | S | /ʂ/ | Retroflex fricative |
| ñ or ny | N | /ɲ/ | Palatal nasal |
| ly | L | /lʲ/ | Palatalized lateral |
| ts | ts | /ts/ | Alveolar affricate |
| ty | tY | /tʲ/ | Palatal stop |
| č or tc | c | /tʃ/ | Postalveolar affricate |
| ' or ʼ | ʼ | /ʔ/ | Glottal stop |
| ā, ē, etc. | aa, ee, etc. | /aː, eː/ | Long vowels (macron → doubling) |

### 3.2 How Arroyo de la Cuesta Wrote What He Heard

Arroyo used Spanish orthographic conventions in the early 1800s:

| Arroyo | Modern Mutsun | IPA | Notes |
|--------|---------------|-----|-------|
| ch | c | /tʃ/ | Spanish convention |
| x | h (or x) | /h/ or /x/ | Colonial Spanish convention |
| tc | c | /tʃ/ | Affricate |
| qu | k | /k/ | Before front vowels |
| ñ | N | /ɲ/ | Standard Spanish |

### 3.3 How Kroeber Wrote What He Heard

Kroeber (1904, 1910) used another variant system:

| Kroeber | Modern | IPA | Notes |
|---------|--------|-----|-------|
| tc | c | /tʃ/ | |
| c | k | /k/ | Before back vowels |
| C | S | /ʂ/ or /ʃ/ | For postalveolar/retroflex |

## 5. Ethical Guidelines for Archive Access

1. **Tribal sovereignty:** Ohlone languages belong to Ohlone people. Research should support — never replace — community-led revitalization.

2. **Access restrictions:** Some materials have restrictions placed by tribal communities. Respect these unconditionally.

3. **Prior informed consent:** Before publishing or sharing any materials derived from Ohlone language data, consult with the relevant tribal language committees.

4. **Citation:** Always cite both the original recorder AND the native speaker. These speakers gave their knowledge to ensure their languages would survive.

5. **Reciprocity:** If your research produces tools, databases, or resources useful for language revitalization, share them with the tribal communities.

6. **No commodification:** The CC BY-NC 4.0 license on the Mutsun dictionary explicitly prohibits commercial use. Respect this for all Ohlone language materials.
